# The 30-Minute Film Session
### The GameFilmNow Coach's Playbook — how to run film like the pros, sized for Little League

> Pros watch film. College watches film. High school watches film.
> Your kids can too — in 30 minutes a week, without losing a single one of them to boredom.

---

## Before the season: set the rhythm

- Pick your film day (most teams: Tuesday, since film lands within 24h of a weekend game).
- Tell parents at the season meeting: *"Film homework is part of this team. It's 10 minutes on a phone. It's how the big boys get better."*
- Get every player a Player Pass (track it on your dashboard — you can't run film with half a roster locked out).

## The 30-minute session (run it the same every week)

**Minutes 0–5 — The Wins.**
Open with 3–4 plays you tagged as "we did this RIGHT." Kids lean in when film starts with them looking good. Ask *"why did this work?"* — make a kid say it out loud.

**Minutes 5–20 — The Teach (max 8 plays).**
Pick ONE theme per week — not everything that went wrong. Blocking landmarks, alignment, pursuit angles — one. Pull only plays showing that theme. For each: play it once at speed, once slow, ask a question BEFORE the reveal ("freeze — where should 55's helmet be?"), let players answer first, then coach it. 8 plays max. Past 8, you've lost 10-year-olds.

**Minutes 20–27 — The Homework Reveal.**
Post next week's film question in the app right there in front of them (e.g. pin Play 14: *"What hole should we hit on this look? Everyone answers by Friday."*). They watch you post it, so nobody can say they didn't know. The app tracks who answered — the tracker is on your coach dashboard, and reminder nudges go to parents with one tap.

**Minutes 27–30 — The Highlight.**
End on the single best play of the game. Let them be loud about it. Last emotion wins — leave the room hyped, not corrected.

## How to review a whole game in 30 minutes of YOUR prep time

1. Watch the play list straight through on 1.5× (~15 min for a Little League game). Don't take notes yet.
2. Second pass, tag as you go using likes + your list: ❤ = wins reel, 📁 My List = teach reel. (~10 min)
3. Pick the week's ONE theme from what My List is telling you. Post the homework question. (~5 min)

## Homework that actually gets done

- One question, one play, answerable in one sentence.
- Ask "what/where/why" questions, never "who messed up" — the film room is safe or it's dead.
- Kids who answer get called on first at the next session (status, not candy).
- Position-group tags (@RunningBacks, @OLine) make homework feel like belonging, not chores.

## Turning film into practice (the loop that makes you a better coach)

Film theme → Tuesday session → **the same theme becomes Wednesday's first two drills** → tag the fix on next week's film → show the before/after clips back-to-back. A kid watching himself fix something in seven days believes in practice forever. That before/after pair is also fundraising gold — parents share it, and improvement sells next season by itself.

## Individual development (5 extra minutes per week)

- Each player tags their own plays — require 3 self-tags per game ("find yourself doing something right, find yourself doing something to fix").
- End of season, every kid has a season archive and can build their highlight tape — that's YOUR alumni pipeline and your tryout-week gift to the next level's coaches.
- Send one voice-note style comment to one different player's play each week. One sentence from coach on a kid's own film is worth a whole practice of yelling.

## Don'ts

- Don't run film past 35 minutes. Ever.
- Don't show a kid's mistake without showing the same kid a win in the same session.
- Don't coach three themes — you'll land zero.
- Don't skip a week; the habit is the product.
