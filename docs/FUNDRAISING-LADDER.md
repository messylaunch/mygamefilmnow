# The GameFilmNow Fundraising Ladder
### The team handout — print this, walk every team through it on a 30-minute call

> Everybody sells candy. Nobody buys candy because they want candy — they buy it to be nice, once.
> You're not selling candy anymore. **You're selling the game.** People actually want that, every single week.

---

## Rung 1 — The List (week 1, before anything else)

Every player writes down **the 20 people who'd actually want to watch them play**: grandparents, aunts/uncles, godparents, mom's coworkers, dad's barber. Not strangers — people who already love this kid.

- Use the Top-20 sheet in the app (parents enter/approve the contacts).
- 22 players × 20 names = **440 people who care about your team.** That's your fundraiser. It was sitting in everyone's phone the whole time.
- The system automatically invites them, reminds them on game week, and hands them the stream link. Nobody on your team has to remember to text anybody.

**Your only job:** get every player's sheet filled by Friday. Track it on your coach dashboard (it shows who's done).

## Rung 2 — Game Week, Every Week (automatic)

Once the lists are in, every game sells itself:
- Tuesday: everyone's list gets "🏈 {Team} plays Saturday — watch live" with the link.
- Game day: "Kickoff in 2 hours" reminder.
- After: "Game film is ready — own it forever."

**Your only job:** confirm your game rows (time, price, stream window) are right in the schedule by Monday night. 5 minutes.

## Rung 3 — Sell the Game to Businesses (weeks 2–4)

It's easier to sell a game to a business owner than a box of chocolate. The pitch, word for word:

> "Hi, I coach the {Team} — {player on your team} is one of ours. Our games stream live to a few hundred local families every week, and our team page is where they all go to watch. A sponsor slot on that page is $___ for the season — your logo in front of every family, every week, and it all goes to the kids. Want to be on there by Friday's game?"

- 3 slots × $250–500/season is realistic for a first season. That's real money, and the businesses get real eyeballs.
- You keep 100% of sponsor money. GFN takes no cut — we just gave you the ad space.
- Bring a phone, show them the team page, close on the spot with the QR code.

**Your only job:** pick 10 businesses within a mile of your field. Get 3.

## Rung 4 — The Big Push (rivalry games, tournaments, senior night)

2–3 times a season, stack it:
- Bundle push: "Stream + keep the recording forever, $14.99."
- Merch on the team page next to the game everyone's buying anyway.
- Your extra 300-contact team list (boosters, car-wash signups, door-to-door) gets the big-game blast.
- Tournament? Every family buys the tournament pass once instead of deciding game by game.

## Rung 5 — Count It, Show It, Repeat It

- Your dashboard shows revenue per game and per season. Screenshot it. Post it (the number, not the names): *"Our families raised $2,300 this season just by watching our games."*
- Players see tickets sold (never dollars) — let them compete on it. "Who fills their Top-20 first" is a better fundraiser kickoff than any candy assembly.
- Next season starts with the lists already built.

## The math (show this to your team parents at the season meeting)

| | Candy fundraiser | GameFilmNow season |
|---|---|---|
| What you sell | $2 chocolate, once | 10+ games, all season |
| Who sells it | Kids, door to door | The system, automatically |
| Realistic take | $300–600, one weekend of pain | $1,500–3,000+ passive |
| What families get | Chocolate | Every game, live and forever |
| What the team gets | Nothing else | Film study, highlights, recruiting profiles |

**Rules that make it work:** every player fills their 20 · confirm your schedule weekly · ask 10 businesses, land 3 · do the film homework so families see the kids improving — improvement sells next season by itself.
