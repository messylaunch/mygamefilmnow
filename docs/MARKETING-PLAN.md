# GameFilmNow Marketing Game Plan

## 1. Positioning (say this everywhere, the same way)

**One-liner:** *GameFilmNow is the fundraiser that actually works — because instead of candy, your team sells the game itself.*

Three pillars, in the order you lead with them:

1. **The fundraiser that beats candy.** Everybody sells chocolate, sweaters, car washes — with zero connection to the actual games. We sell the moment: watch your kid live from anywhere. Easier to sell a game to a business owner than a candy box — and we coach teams through exactly how (that's the moat: **coaching, not just software**).
2. **The study tool this level is missing.** Pros watch film. College watches film. High school watches film. If your league doesn't, your kids are getting left behind. Film back in 24 hours, play-by-play, with coach questions built in.
3. **Every connection counts.** Showing up is great; watching from Ohio is great too; even subscribing to a kid's replays tells that kid someone cares. We bring game day to life — multi-camera film, photos, pre-game moments, crowd energy — and guarantee every play on camera (not a phone on a fence).

**Who we talk to, and what they hear:**
| Audience | Lead message |
|---|---|
| Coaches/team managers | "Your fundraiser is broken. This one pays every week and makes your team better." |
| Parents/family | "Never miss another game — and grandma won't either." |
| League directors | "Bring pro-level game day to your whole league; we handle everything." |
| Local businesses | "Sponsor a team page real families look at every week — not a banner nobody reads." |

## 2. The ladder (walk every client up it — this is the retention engine)

Rung 1 → **Film the games** (the sale that gets you in the door)
Rung 2 → **Teach them to sell it** (`FUNDRAISING-LADDER.md` — the handout + your coaching call). Teams that hand the system off with no coaching make no money and churn. Teams you coach, stay.
Rung 3 → **Make the team better** (`COACHES-PLAYBOOK.md` — the 30-minute film session; now the coach personally needs you)
Rung 4 → **Own the season** (player profiles, highlight tapes, sponsors, merch — now leaving GFN means the kids lose their film history)
Rung 5 → **Expand** (their tournament invites other teams → those teams see the cameras and the QR codes → new leads)

## 3. Offer stack

- **Front-end offer (to teams):** "First game filmed free — if your families don't buy it, you owe nothing." Costs you one crew visit; removes all risk; the stream sales at game one usually close the season deal.
- **Core:** season filming contract (custom quote) OR Camera School partner (one-time season fee + equipment-scaled pricing).
- **Upsells:** Player Passes ($75) → Highlight Mode ($40) → team merch/sponsor slots (team keeps sponsor money — this SELLS the platform, it doesn't monetize it for you; the monetization is retention).
- **Fan side:** stream ($9.99) < bundle ($14.99) < team pass ($49.99) < tournament pass — anchor pricing on the game page does the selling.

## 4. Channels (in order — don't spread thin)

1. **Field presence** (your unfair advantage): every game you film is a live demo. QR-code signage at the field → start. page. Announce "this game is live on GameFilmNow — grandma's watching" over the PA.
2. **Facebook groups + local FB ads**: league parent groups are where Little League actually lives. Post the free-first-game offer + highlight clips (with parent permission). $5–10/day geo-targeted ads on game-day reminder creative.
3. **Direct to coaches**: DM/email using the ladder pitch. The fundraising angle opens doors the "video service" angle doesn't.
4. **Local businesses**: sell sponsor slots FOR your teams (you're their fundraising coach, remember) — every slot you help sell proves rung 2 and locks the team in.
5. **The kids themselves**: Top-20 lists + referral texts (GHL flow E) — every player becomes a distribution node. 22 kids × 20 contacts = 440 warm leads per team.

## 5. Content calendar (repeatable weekly loop)

| Day | Post |

| Wed | Fundraising win ("The Eagles raised $410 this week without selling a single candy bar") |

| Fri–Sun | LIVE badges, stream links, final scores, "recording drops Monday" |

## 6. Numbers to watch (one dashboard, five numbers)

1. Streams+recordings sold per game (target: 25+ by week 3 of a team's season)
2. Player Pass attach rate (target: 70% of roster)
3. Team revenue per season (the number that renews contracts — put it in the coach's face weekly)
4. Support tickets per 100 orders (the old build's silent killer; target <2)
5. Referral signups from Top-20 texts (the growth loop)

## 7. What NOT to do

- Don't sell "video service" — sell fundraiser + development. Video is the delivery mechanism.
- Don't onboard a team without scheduling their fundraising coaching call — uncoached teams churn.
- Don't chase leagues before 3 reference teams have revenue screenshots.
- Don't let kids' faces into ads without signed media release (fold it into the parent consent form).
