01

Start here — what this platform actually is

GameFilmNow does three jobs for your team: it puts every game on camera (live streams + recordings your fans buy), it gives your players a pro-style film room (every play, cut and ready in 24 hours), and it turns both into a fundraiser that actually pays — because you're selling the game itself, not candy.

The money side and the football side feed each other: teams that study film play better, teams that play better sell more film, and the sales fund the program. This workbook walks both tracks at once.

02

Coach setup — tutorial

Set up your season in one sitting

Open Coach → My Schedule

Every game is one row: date, time, opponent, stream window, prices. Type in the Opponent box and matching GFN teams appear — pick one, or create a new opponent on the spot.

Set each game's stream window

Start and end times. The stream goes on sale automatically before kickoff and comes OFF sale at the end time — you never take anything down by hand.

Set your prices

Stream, recording, and bundle per game. Typical: $9.99 stream / $7.99 recording / $14.99 bundle. Your Team Pass covers the whole season.

Invite your players

Coach → Team Page → Invite players. Enter each player's name and their parent's email and phone. GFN sends the Player Pass invite and the parent consent form — kids are never contacted directly. No consent, no film room.

Dress your public page

Bio, roster, sponsor slots, merch. This page is your storefront — every fan who buys a ticket sees it.

Fixing things

Game time changed? Edit the row — every fan who bought gets the update automatically. Game cancelled? Tell GFN support and every purchase refunds in one batch; buyers get a text. Score wrong? Type the right one in the Score cell.

Player basics — tutorial

Open Film from the bottom bar

Fresh film lands within 24 hours of every game, already cut play by play. Tap any cut to watch; Next/Prev or the strip to move.

❤ Like and ⊕ save

Heart the plays you love; add plays to My List to study later. Two taps, like the apps you already use.

🏷️ Tag yourself — this is the big one

Tag every play you're in (aim for 3+ per game: one you're proud of, one to fix, one hustle play). Tags build your personal reel and your season archive — they follow you team to team, year to year.

Talk football with @

Type @ in a comment to search teammates by name or position and tag them. Ask coach questions right on the play. It's a study space, not social media — everything stays with the team.

Answer the homework

When coach pins a question to a play, it hits your notifications. Answer before the film session — coach sees who did.

04

The 30-minute film session — the coach's system

Pros watch film. College watches film. Your team can too — in 30 minutes a week, without losing a single kid to boredom. Run it the same every week:

  1. Minutes 0–5 — the wins. Open with 3–4 plays you tagged as "we did this RIGHT." Ask "why did this work?" and make a kid say it out loud.
  2. Minutes 5–20 — the teach (8 plays max). ONE theme per week — blocking landmarks, pursuit angles, alignment. Play it at speed, play it slow, ask the question BEFORE the reveal, let players answer first.
  3. Minutes 20–27 — the homework reveal. Pin next week's question in the app in front of them, so nobody can say they didn't know. The tracker shows who answered.
  4. Minutes 27–30 — the highlight. End on the best play of the game and let them be loud. Last emotion wins.

Your prep in 30 minutes flat

First pass at 1.5× speed, no notes. Second pass: ❤ the wins reel, ⊕ My List the teach reel. Pick the week's one theme from what My List tells you, post the homework question, done.

The loop that changes teams: film theme → Tuesday session → the same theme becomes Wednesday's first two drills → tag the fix on next week's film → show the before/after pair. A kid watching himself fix something in seven days believes in practice forever.

05

Player growth — from tags to a tryout tape

Every tag is a brick. By season's end a player who tags 3 plays a game has a 30+ play archive — and that's a highlight tape waiting to happen.

  1. All season: tag proud / fix / hustle every game. The "fix" tags matter most — pull last month's fix tags and watch the improvement on film. Show a parent. Show a scout.
  2. Streaks keep it honest: the study streak on your home screen counts days you opened film. Protect the streak.
  3. End of season: Highlight Mode unlocks the reel builder — pick your best 20, choose a soundtrack, and the reel builds itself. Download every tagged play as files, no screen recording ever.
  4. The tape goes public on your profile — middle school and high school coaches can watch it without an account. Rec league kids almost never show up to tryouts with film. Yours will.

Profit play #1 — the Top-20 engine

The single highest-leverage thing your team will do all season happens in week one and takes 15 minutes per family: every player lists the 20 people who'd actually want to watch them play. Grandparents, aunts and uncles, godparents, mom's coworkers, dad's barber.

22 players × 20 names = 440 people who already love someone on your team. That's your fundraiser — it was sitting in everyone's phones the whole time. Once the lists are in, the system invites them, reminds them every game week, and hands them the stream link. Nobody has to remember to text anybody, ever.

Profit play #2 — sell the game to businesses

It's easier to sell a game to a business owner than a box of chocolate. Your team page has sponsor slots — that's ad space real local families look at every single week, and you keep 100% of sponsor money.

The script (say it word for word)

"Hi, I coach the {Team} — {player they know} is one of ours. Our games stream live to a few hundred local families every week, and our team page is where they all go to watch. A sponsor slot on that page is ${___} for the season — your logo in front of every family, every week, and it all goes to the kids. Want to be up there by Friday's game?"

  • Bring your phone. Show them the team page. Close on the spot.
  • 3 slots × $250–500 a season is realistic for a first year.
  • Pick the 10 businesses closest to your field. You need 3 yeses.

Profit play #3 — the game-week machine & big-game pushes

Every week, automatically

Tuesday: everyone's Top-20 gets "{Team} plays Saturday — watch live" with the link. Game day: "kickoff in 2 hours." After: "the film is ready — own it forever." Your only job: confirm the game row (time, price, stream window) is right by Monday night. Five minutes.

2–3 times a season, stack it

  • Rivalry and tournament games: push the bundle ("watch live now, keep it forever — $14.99").
  • Merch next to the game: hoodies and tees sell best the week everyone's already buying the big game.
  • Your extra 300-contact team list (boosters, car-wash signups, door-to-door) gets the big-game blast — that's what it's for.
  • Tournaments: families buy the Tournament Pass once instead of deciding game by game — pitch it before round one.

Quick answers

  • When does film arrive? Team play-by-play within 24 hours; public recording on sale within 72.
  • A parent can't find their purchase. It's saved under My Games in their account, and the link was texted to them at purchase. It never expires.
  • Game cancelled — refunds? One batch refund for the whole game via GFN support; every buyer gets notified automatically.
  • Can a kid buy things? No. Kid-initiated purchases (like Highlight Mode) send an approval request to the parent — kids never enter card details.
  • Player on two teams? The pill at the top-left of the app switches teams — film, homework, and calendar re-scope instantly.
  • Who sees the revenue? Coaches see dollars. Players see tickets sold only.
  • Is the film room public? No — it's the team's private study space, behind the Player Pass and parent consent. Only the highlight montage a player chooses to publish is public.
  • Texts to kids? Never. All texts and emails go to parents; kids get in-app notifications only.

Still stuck? Ask your GFN rep, or book a fundraising strategy call — coaching is part of what you're paying for.
